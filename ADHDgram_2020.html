Dear [FIRST_NAME/friends],


It’s here again: the restless feeling about the state of my house that always precedes a complete blow-up.

I let clutter build up around the holidays. I’ve put off projects — like repainting my living room — because I know they’ll be a pain in the neck. It’s gone on too long.

I know the drill by now: I’ll get completely fed up, rant and rave about it to my family on a Saturday afternoon, then channel my anger into huge progress. Clutter will leave the house by the carton. Dirty nooks will get scrubbed with a toothbrush. I’ll brush off the flaking paint and put a coat of primer on the nasty baseboard behind the living room couch. I’ll sew and hang new curtains, pay a carpenter to make a pretty cover for the kitchen radiator, and dig up the back garden.

And I’ll probably swear I’m turning over a new leaf. No more letting these projects build up until I feel humiliated and rage-y about them.

That won’t last. Nothing ever does. I’ll go through another busy time and the backlog will return, kicking off a fresh round of the cycle.

It’s easy for ADHDers to get cynical about this. Harder to accept this reality and work with it rather than against it. But accept it we must, as I insist in Rule #1 in Order from Chaos. We can either spend our energy fighting our brains — and our reality — or figuring out a way to swim with the current.

This month I’m anticipating a massive current of discontent and preparing myself to swim with it as far downstream as it’ll take me.

I won’t fight the restlessness this time. I’ll open the door, let it in, and harness its energy to get a few pesky jobs accomplished.

Then I’ll let it go peacefully when the time comes. I’ll accept that I didn’t accomplish everything I wanted to (probably because I wanted too much for the time I had available) and that I need to move on to other priorities I’ve surely neglected. That inspiration will return. It always does.

Sometimes we need to force ourselves through something. Other times it’s not life-or-death and we can benefit from working with, not against, our natural inclinations.

I’m thankful for the wisdom to accept that this is one of those times.

xoxo,
